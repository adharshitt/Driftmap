Here are features you can build specifically to verify the system works and prove its value:

---

**Live Health Indicators**
Add a status bar in the TUI showing packets captured per second, ring buffer utilization %, and active TCP streams. If the number is 0, something is broken. Makes internals visible at a glance.

**Self-Test Mode**
`driftmap selftest` — spins up two tiny internal HTTP servers, sends 100 known requests, and verifies scores come back correct. Lets any user confirm the tool works before pointing it at real traffic. Critical for trust.

**Drift Replay**
`driftmap replay <session-id>` — re-runs a captured divergent pair through the scoring engine again. Useful for verifying a bug fix actually resolved the drift without needing live traffic.

**Baseline Snapshot**
`driftmap snapshot --save baseline` then `driftmap snapshot --compare baseline` — lets you capture a known-good state and diff against it later. Proves the system is tracking change over time, not just instantaneous state.

**Divergence Alerts via CLI Exit Code**
`driftmap watch --fail-above 5.0` — exits with a non-zero code if any endpoint exceeds 5% drift score. Makes Driftmap directly usable in CI/CD pipelines as a release gate.

**Endpoint Summary Report**
`driftmap report --format json` — dumps a machine-readable summary of all endpoint scores. Lets you pipe results into other tools, Slack bots, dashboards.

**Request Sampling Inspector**
`driftmap inspect --endpoint "POST /api/orders" --sample 10` — shows 10 actual request/response pairs that contributed to a drift score, side by side. The most important debugging feature — without it you know *that* something drifted but not *why*.

**Score History Graph**
In the TUI, show a sparkline of each endpoint's drift score over the last 60 minutes. A score that spikes and recovers tells a very different story than one that slowly climbs.

**Normalization Dry Run**
`driftmap normalize --test "your-json-here"` — shows exactly what the normalizer strips from a given JSON payload before scoring. Lets users verify their custom normalization rules actually work.

**Equivalent Service Guarantee**
Point Driftmap at the same service twice (target-a and target-b are identical). Score must be 0.0% across all endpoints. If it's not, the scoring engine has a bug. This is the most important correctness check you can ship.
